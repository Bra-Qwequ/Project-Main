lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian baked casserole dish made by layering wide, flat pasta sheets with various fillings.

It is considered one of the oldest forms of pasta, with origins tracing back to ancient Greece and Rome, though it became a staple of Italian cuisine in Naples during the Middle Ages.

Steps in Preparing Lasagna

  1. Preheat Oven: Set your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Prepare Meat Sauce: Brown ground beef and Italian sausage in a pan. Add diced onions and garlic, cooking until softened. Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, and Italian seasonings (basil, oregano, salt, and pepper), then simmer to thicken.
  3. Cook Noodles: Boil a large pot of salted water and cook lasagna noodles until al dente (usually 8 to 10 minutes), then drain. Skip this step if using oven-ready noodles.
  4. Prepare Cheese Filling: In a medium bowl, combine ricotta cheese with a large egg (acts as a binder), Parmesan cheese, and fresh herbs like parsley.
  5. Base Layer: Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish to prevent sticking.
  6. First Pasta Layer: Place a single layer of lasagna noodles over the sauce.
  7. Repeat Layering: Spread a portion of the ricotta mixture over the noodles, followed by meat sauce and a sprinkling of shredded mozzarella cheese. Repeat these layers until the dish is nearly full.
  8. Final Top Layer: Top the final layer of pasta with the remaining meat sauce and a generous amount of mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  9. Bake: Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake at 350°F–375°F for about 25–30 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10–15 minutes until the cheese is golden and bubbling.
  10. Rest: Let the lasagna rest for 10–15 minutes before slicing to allow the layers to set.
